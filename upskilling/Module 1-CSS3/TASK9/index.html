Council Announces Expansion of Green Spaces Across the City

By Ramesh Nair | June 14, 2026

The city council held a landmark press conference on Thursday morning, announcing a sweeping initiative to expand public green spaces in every ward of the city. The plan, dubbed "Green Horizons 2030", aims to add over 200 new parks, gardens, and community nature zones within the next four years.

Mayor Sunita Reddy addressed a large gathering at Town Hall, emphasising that access to green space is a fundamental right for all citizens, regardless of which neighbourhood they call home. "We want every child to grow up with a park within walking distance," she said.

The initiative will be funded through a combination of central government grants, private partnerships with real-estate developers, and a dedicated "Green Tax" surcharge on commercial construction projects exceeding 10,000 square feet. Critics from the industry have already raised concerns about the surcharge, calling it a disincentive for investment.

Environmental groups, however, have welcomed the move with cautious optimism. The Nature Trust of India's city chapter stated that while the targets are ambitious, the council must pair expansion with active maintenance and biodiversity goals — not simply turfing over concrete.

Ward councillors are expected to submit shortlisted sites by August, with ground-breaking ceremonies for the first batch of parks scheduled for Republic Day, January 2027. Residents are encouraged to participate in the public consultation process, which opens online on 1 July 2026.

Community Volunteers Log Record Hours in May

By Lakshmi Priya | June 10, 2026

May 2026 has been the most productive month for community volunteering on record. Over 1,400 registered volunteers contributed a combined 8,200 hours to local initiatives ranging from beach clean-ups and food drives to skill-sharing workshops.

The Volunteer Coordination Cell, which tracks hours and projects, said that participation surged particularly among the 18–25 age group, driven largely by social media campaigns run by the Youth Council's "Give Back" movement.

Priya Sharma, named Volunteer of the Month, helped organise three youth sports days, two coding workshops, and a career mentorship programme entirely in her spare time. She credited her inspiration to a motivational event she attended at the Community Centre two years ago.

The Coordination Cell plans to launch a digital badge system in July, allowing volunteers to showcase verified hours on professional networking platforms. Applications open on 15 July for the new Volunteer Leadership Programme, which provides training in event management and civic communication.
